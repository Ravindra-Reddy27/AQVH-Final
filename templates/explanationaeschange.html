BB84 Quantum Key Distribution Protocol

A step-by-step guide to understanding quantum cryptography's fundamental protocol

What is BB84?

The BB84 protocol, developed by Bennett and Brassard in 1984, is the first quantum cryptography protocol. It enables two parties (traditionally called Alice and Bob) to generate a shared secret key using quantum properties of photons.

Key Concepts:

  • Quantum States: Information is encoded in quantum states (photon polarization)
  • Two Bases: Rectilinear (+) and Diagonal (√ó) measurement bases are used
  • No-Cloning Theorem: Quantum states cannot be perfectly copied
  • Measurement Impact: Measuring in the wrong basis disturbs the state

Protocol Steps

1. Quantum Transmission

Alice sends qubits to Bob, randomly choosing between:

2. Measurement

Bob measures each qubit, randomly choosing either basis (+) or (√ó)

3. Basis Reconciliation

Alice and Bob publicly share which bases they used (but not the bits)

4. Key Sifting

They keep only the bits where they used the same basis
